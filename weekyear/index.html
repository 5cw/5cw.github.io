This is an incomplete unserious proposal for fun.

The year is divided into 7 months

Each month is divided into 7 or 8 weeks

Weeks are divided into the standard 7 days.

Years are grouped into 7-Year "Cycles,"

Cycles are grouped into 7-Cycle "Periods,"

Periods are grouped into "Ages,"

Ages are grouped into "Superdays,"

Superdays are grouped into "Superweeks,"

Superages are grouped into "Supersuperyears,"

and so on, adding more prefixes of Super- until society collapses, we decide to start a new calendar from scratch, or both.


Frimonth, Saturmonth, and Sunmonth always have an Endweek.

(this is also about as much drift as the Gregorian calendar has at present, and could be left out if you are pessimistic about the age of humanity)

Most years are 364 days, some are 371 days, about every 2 centuries a year is 378 days, and about once every couple million years a year is 385 days.


A day of the year can be written using their first or other letters or numbers which represent their corresponding units of time.

Thurs-units and Sun-units can be written with H or R and U or N respectively (to disambiguate them from Tues-units and Satur-units).

So Sunday, Thursweek, Tuesmonth would be NRT or 631. Monday, Endweek, Frimonth would be MEF or 074. There's no need for separators

because they can't take up more than one digit. The numerical representation can thus be thought of as a right to left mixed-radix senary/octal number, so Mon-units are 0.


Monday, Monweek, Monmonth, Monyear 0000 (omitting digits/units to the right defaults to Mon-units) is pegged to Monday, September 3rd, 1894 in the Gregorian calendar.
